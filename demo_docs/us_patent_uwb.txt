PATENT SPECIFICATION
Patent Number: US-1182734-A1
Title: Multi-Agent Drone Swarm Coordination via UWB Ranging
Inventors: Dr. Marcus Vance, MIT Robotics Lab
Year: 2023

SUMMARY:
A method for coordinating multiple UAV agents in a decentralized swarm without centralized GPS. Drones calculate mutual distances dynamically using ultra-wideband (UWB) time-of-flight pulses and apply virtual-spring forces to maintain formation.

DETAILED DESCRIPTION & CLAIMS:
What is claimed is:
1. A method for relative spatial coordination among a plurality of unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) in a swarm, comprising:
   - Transmitting ultra-wideband (UWB) pulses between at least three nodes mounted on different UAV chassis in the swarm;
   - Calculating mutual time-of-flight ranges between each of the nodes in real-time;
   - Applying a decentralized virtual-spring force mathematical model locally on each UAV processor;
   - Dynamically recalculating a collision-free trajectory vector for each UAV to maintain relative distance without centralized GPS coordinates.
